Plus ROI framing and objection handling.

## 1) One-Page Executive Summary

**The problem**
Maintenance and facility issues at our properties live in too many places — RoomMaster
for housekeeping status, Eptura for assets, and phone calls, texts, and paper for
everything in between. Issues fall through the cracks, rooms sit out-of-service longer
than they should, and no one has a single live picture of what's happening across a
property.

**The opportunity**
Out-of-service room-nights are lost revenue, and slow maintenance shows up in guest
reviews. A faster, more visible maintenance operation directly protects revenue and
guest satisfaction.

**The solution — FacilityFlow**
A single operational command center for a property:
- **Live floor plans** — every room color-coded by status, top-down, at a glance
- **Work orders** — create, assign, photo-document, and close issues with full history
- **Role-based access** — front desk, housekeeping, and maintenance each see exactly what they need
- **Integrations** — syncs with the **RoomMaster and Eptura** we already use (doesn't replace them)
- **Real-time team chat** and **reports** that surface where issues cluster

**Why it fits Coral**
- Built around our actual stack (RoomMaster + Eptura)
- Multi-property by design — each property's data is fully isolated and secure
- Already loaded and running with **real Amicalola Falls data** as a working proof of concept

**The ask**
A **60-day pilot at Amicalola Falls**, run alongside current tools, measuring average
work-order resolution time and out-of-service room-nights. Low risk, fully measurable.

## 3) Five-Minute Live Demo Script

> Log in ahead of time as `manager@amicalolafalls.com`. Have the live URL open.

1. **Dashboard (45s)** — "This is the whole property at a glance — active issues, room
   health, who's online. Every number is clickable." Click **Active Issues** → it jumps
   to the work order list.
2. **Floor plan (60s)** — Open **Amicalola Falls Lodge**. "This is the building, top-down.
   Green is good, red needs attention." Click the emergency room (306) → show the burst-pipe
   work order tied to it. Mention the **Edit Layout** builder: "We can map any property by
   drawing rooms on the grid."
3. **RoomMaster sync (45s)** — On the dashboard, click **Sync Now** on the RoomMaster panel.
   "It just pulled housekeeping statuses — dirty rooms flipped to 'cleaning required' and it
   auto-created the work orders. Two-way sync with what we already use."
4. **Work order flow (60s)** — Open a work order. Move it along the progress bar, assign a
   technician, post an update, attach a photo. "Full history, photo evidence, nothing lost."
5. **Roles (30s)** — Settings → Roles & Permissions. "Front desk, housekeeping, maintenance
   — each role sees only what it should. Fully configurable."
6. **Close (20s)** — "Everything you just saw is live data, isolated per property, and it
   runs alongside RoomMaster and Eptura. I'd like to pilot it here at Amicalola for 60 days."

## Objection Handling

- **"Who supports this / is it a real product?"**
  Be honest: it's an early-stage tool, proposed as a measured pilot — not a signed vendor
  contract yet. That's exactly why we start with 60 days at one property.
- **"We already have RoomMaster and Eptura."**
  This doesn't replace them — it sits on top and syncs with both, giving the unified live
  view neither one provides on its own.
- **"Is our data secure?"**
  Each property's data is fully isolated (row-level security), encrypted in transit and at
  rest, with role-based access. Happy to walk IT through it.
- **"What does it cost / what's the lift to roll out?"**
  The pilot needs almost no setup — a property's rooms can be mapped in an afternoon, and it
  reads from the RoomMaster/Eptura we already run.
